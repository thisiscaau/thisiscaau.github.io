Definition of primitive root

G is the primitive root modulo n if and only if for any integer a such that gcd(a,n) = 1, there exist some k that : g^k - a is divisible by n.

k is then called the index or discrete logarithm of a to the base g modulo n. G is also called the generator of multiplicative group of integers modulo n.

In particular, for the case where n is a prime number, the powers of primitive root runs through all numbers from 1 to n - 1.

Existence of primitive root

Primitive root modulo n exists if and only if:

  • n is 1,2,4
  • n is power of an odd prime number
  • n = 2 * (p ^ k) (p is prime)
